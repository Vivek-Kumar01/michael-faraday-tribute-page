Michael Faraday

"Michael Faraday, a pioneer who turned the sparks of curiosity into the lightning of innovation."

Milestones in the Life of Michael Faraday

  • 1791 - Born on September 22 in Newington Butts, England.
  • 1805 - Apprenticed to a local bookbinder and bookseller, George Riebau, for seven years.
  • 1812 - Attended lectures by the eminent chemist Sir Humphry Davy at the Royal Institution.
  • 1813 - Hired as a laboratory assistant by Sir Humphry Davy at the Royal Institution.
  • 1813-1815 - Accompanied Davy on a European tour, meeting prominent scientists and gaining valuable experience.
  • 1821 - Made his first major discovery in electromagnetism: electromagnetic rotation, the principle behind the electric motor.
  • 1823 - Liquefied chlorine gas, demonstrating that gases could be turned into liquids.
  • 1825 - Discovered benzene, an important organic chemical compound.
  • 1831 - Discovered electromagnetic induction, the principle behind the electric transformer and generator.
  • 1836 - Introduced the concept of the magnetic field in his studies of electromagnetism.
  • 1839 - Began experimenting with electrolysis, leading to the formulation of Faraday's laws of electrolysis.
  • 1845 - Discovered the Faraday effect, showing that light and magnetism are related.
  • 1855 - Health began to decline, leading to reduced scientific work.
  • 1861 - Attended the inauguration of the Faraday Laboratory at the Royal Institution.
  • 1867 - Passed away on August 25 in Hampton Court, Surrey, England.
